Case Study

Beyond Recall: Authentic Assessment in the AI Era

Summary

If tasks reward recall and format, students will outsource to AI. Redesigning assessment toward explanation, creation and dialogue preserves learning value even when AI is present. Oral defences, in‑class builds, portfolios and iterative projects make understanding visible and reduce incentives to copy.

Factors

Students are more motivated by meaningful tasks that reflect real practice. Staff need time and tools to redesign assessments. Digital submissions reduce printing and travel. Integrity frameworks and fair appeals processes must handle suspected misconduct without over‑relying on detectors.

Implications

Authentic tasks build reasoning, communication and collaboration. Graduates become better prepared for AI‑infused workplaces where explanation and judgement matter. Such designs produce adaptable models across contexts.

Solutions

Implement viva voce, in‑class creations and portfolio assessments that foreground explanation and reflection. Refine rubrics to value credible sources, iteration and metacognition. Train staff and students to document legitimate AI use transparently.

Connection to Lecture Topics

Logic for argument quality; Epistemology for evidence and justification; Concept of Human Being for education beyond optimisation.
