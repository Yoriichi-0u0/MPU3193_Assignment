Case Study 2 — AI in the Curriculum (AI Basics by 2027)

Early AI literacy in primary years must build thinking skills, not just tool usage.

Summary

Malaysia plans to teach AI basics in primary schools. Early literacy must be paired with teacher support and assessments that emphasise reasoning and originality.

Context

  • Primary education; AI literacy modules; staged rollout.
  • Teacher training and resource development required.

What Happened

Policy direction set to include foundational AI concepts and ethics for young learners.

Factors

Social

  • Language support and age-appropriate explanations
  • Parent/guardian involvement

Economic

  • Affordable devices and classroom resources

Environmental

  • Reliable power and network in schools

Policy / Governance

  • Curriculum alignment and assessment standards

Implications

Society

Builds shared vocabulary and confidence to work with AI safely.

Country

Supports future workforce readiness and innovation capacity.

Global citizens

Contributes to UNESCO-aligned pushes for AI literacy with ethics at the core.

Solutions

S1. Community Device Libraries

Shared device pools for classes with limited hardware.

S2. Offline-First Learning Packs

Offline-first lesson packs with simple activities and teacher notes.

S3. School Wi-Fi Hubs & Micro-cells

Micro-cells/mesh Wi-Fi to stabilise school connections.

S4. Teacher CPD (UDL + AI Workflows)

Grade-appropriate teacher CPD with classroom exemplars.

S5. Guardrails & Disclosure Policy

Clear rules on disclosure and acceptable use for assignments.

Why This Matters for AI in Education

A strong foundation prevents over-reliance on tools and keeps human understanding central.
