Case Study 1 — MOE AI Guidance for Schools

National guidance informed by pilot schools to steer ethical and effective AI use.

Summary

Malaysia’s MOE is preparing AI literacy/classroom guidance based on pilot schools. Guidance helps teachers use AI for feedback and differentiation without replacing pedagogy or risking integrity.

Context

  • Malaysia — Ministry of Education; pilot schools; guidance in progress.
  • Focus: age-appropriate use, transparency, academic integrity.

What Happened

Pilot experiences informed a draft guideline covering student use, teacher workflows, and assessment integrity.

Factors

Social

  • Teacher workload and confidence with AI tools
  • Parental expectations and transparency

Economic

  • Licensing and device availability
  • Data costs for home access

Environmental

  • Bandwidth variability across regions

Policy / Governance

  • School-level SOPs aligned to national guidance
  • Safeguarding and privacy requirements

Implications

Society

Clear expectations reduce confusion and increase trust between teachers, students, and parents.

Country

Consistent practice across schools improves quality and comparability of outcomes.

Global citizens

Aligns with global moves toward safe, transparent AI use in classrooms.

Solutions

S1. Community Device Libraries

Loaner laptops/tablets so students can access AI-assisted practice at home.

S2. Offline-First Learning Packs

Provide printable/USB packs when bandwidth is limited.

S3. School Wi-Fi Hubs & Micro-cells

After-hours school Wi-Fi access for assignments.

S4. Teacher CPD (UDL + AI Workflows)

Hands-on clinics for prompt design, rubric-aligned feedback, and accessibility.

S5. Guardrails & Disclosure Policy

Disclosure statements and no fully-autonomous grading; human moderation required.

Why This Matters for AI in Education

Guidance ensures AI supports, not replaces, teaching — protecting real understanding and fairness.
