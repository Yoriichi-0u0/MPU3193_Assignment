Study Case

Fairness vs equality of opportunity in school AI

"Your model is only fair if qualified students across groups get the same true positive rate."

Quick take

Schools love early warning dashboards and shiny "at risk" flags, but many models quietly reward advantage. Parent education, postcode, device type, and attendance patterns often act as proxies for wealth. If you predict "who will succeed" without checking group outcomes, you hard code the very gaps you claim to fix. The better question is simple: do equally qualified students across groups get the same chance of being flagged for help or for opportunities?

Run an opportunity audit

A practical fix is to compare true positive rates within the actually qualified set. If one group gets fewer correct positives, you rebalance thresholds, reweight training data, or apply post processing so that equally capable students have equal odds. This is not a theory exercise. Schools already do similar checks for scholarship shortlists and special programs. Make it standard for every model that can change life chances.

Do not stop at a single fairness metric. Track precision, recall, and calibration by subgroup and watch drift term to term. Equality of opportunity becomes a routine just like attendance reconciliation.

Show your work

Report the audit in plain language. Publish a one page model card that lists sensitive features, data refresh cadence, appeal routes, and who signs off on changes. Families should know what feeds the model and how to contest a decision. If a vendor cannot pass a basic opportunity audit or refuses to share documentation, do not deploy. Equity is a design choice, not a press release.

  • List proxy features and justify why they stay.
  • Share subgroup error rates with school councils.
  • Describe how appeals work and who reviews them.
