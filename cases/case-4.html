Case Study 4 — Connectivity First: JENDELA (Sabah & Sarawak)

Bandwidth is the first mile. Without it, AI tools stay theoretical.

Summary

Upgrades expand coverage and speed in East Malaysia. Reliable access enables LMS, video feedback, and AI-assisted practice.

Context

  • Sabah & Sarawak; rural and remote communities; school access.
  • Progressive coverage increases; local school Wi-Fi roles.

What Happened

Network improvements allow schools to schedule after-hours study and downloads.

Factors

Social

  • Community study spaces and safety

Economic

  • Subsidised data for education domains

Environmental

  • Terrain, weather, and power stability

Policy / Governance

  • Public-private partnerships and school hub policies

Implications

Society

More equitable access to digital resources and collaboration.

Country

Raises baseline quality and reduces regional disparities.

Global citizens

Demonstrates infrastructure-led inclusion strategies.

Solutions

S1. Community Device Libraries

Local device libraries at schools/libraries.

S2. Offline-First Learning Packs

USB packs for low-bandwidth households.

S3. School Wi-Fi Hubs & Micro-cells

School hubs and micro-cells focused on study hours.

S4. Teacher CPD (UDL + AI Workflows)

Teacher training to leverage new access fairly.

S5. Guardrails & Disclosure Policy

School policies prioritising educational domains for bandwidth.

Why This Matters for AI in Education

Infrastructure plus pedagogy turns potential into real learning gains.
