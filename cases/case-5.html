Case Study 5 — Refugee Learners & Digital Readiness

Inclusion requires devices, safe platforms, and trained teachers.

Summary

Refugee learners in community centres need better access, teacher support, and safeguarded tools so AI narrows gaps rather than widening them.

Context

  • Alternative/Community-based Learning Centres; NGOs; volunteers.
  • Constraints: devices, connectivity, recognition of learning.

What Happened

Programs focus on readiness, but resource gaps remain; AI must be introduced with care.

Factors

Social

  • Trust, language support, safeguarding

Economic

  • Donations, refurb devices, continuity of funding

Environmental

  • Shared/temporary learning spaces

Policy / Governance

  • Data protection and safeguarding protocols

Implications

Society

Improves continuity of learning for vulnerable groups.

Country

Supports SDG commitments and social cohesion.

Global citizens

Aligns with global right-to-education efforts.

Solutions

S1. Community Device Libraries

Device libraries operated with NGOs and community stewards.

S2. Offline-First Learning Packs

Offline packs in multiple languages with pictorial aids.

S3. School Wi-Fi Hubs & Micro-cells

Portable hotspots / scheduled download windows.

S4. Teacher CPD (UDL + AI Workflows)

Teacher CPD tailored to multilingual, low-resource settings.

S5. Guardrails & Disclosure Policy

Privacy-first platforms with minimal data collection.

Why This Matters for AI in Education

AI should reduce exclusion; design choices must protect dignity and access.
