Case Study 3 — UTM Generative AI Guidelines (Higher Education)

University guardrails show how disclosure and integrity can work in practice.

Summary

UTM’s guidelines require transparency, protect assessment integrity, and position AI as an assistant under human oversight — a model schools can adapt.

Context

  • Higher education; Senate-approved circular; implementation since 2024.
  • References international guidance; campus-wide communication.

What Happened

Guidelines set expectations for students and staff on acceptable uses and disclosures.

Factors

Social

  • Awareness and student buy-in
  • Lecturer marking workflows

Economic

  • Access to permitted tools
  • Time for rubric design

Environmental

  • Network capacity during peak assessment periods

Policy / Governance

  • Academic integrity rules; AI-use statements

Implications

Society

Greater trust in credentials and fairer assessments.

Country

HE exemplars accelerate policy diffusion to schools.

Global citizens

Comparable to international best practice on GenAI in education.

Solutions

S1. Community Device Libraries

Faculty laptop refresh + student loan schemes.

S2. Offline-First Learning Packs

Offline rubrics and print packs when systems are down.

S3. School Wi-Fi Hubs & Micro-cells

Dedicated exam-time bandwidth provisioning.

S4. Teacher CPD (UDL + AI Workflows)

Workshops on AI-aware assessment design and feedback.

S5. Guardrails & Disclosure Policy

Mandatory AI-use disclosure in submissions.

Why This Matters for AI in Education

Clear guardrails enable the benefits of AI while preserving the value of human learning.
