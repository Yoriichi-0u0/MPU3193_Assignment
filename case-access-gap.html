Case Study

Bridging the Gap: Devices, Bandwidth and Language

Summary

AI reduces inequalities only when learners can actually use it. Translation, captions and adaptive pacing support inclusion, but without devices, bandwidth and accessible design, gaps widen. AI must be deployed with an access lens so that quality gains are shared, not stratified.

Factors

Language diversity and disability inclusion matter socially. Economically, device subsidies, lab access and licensing choices are key. Environmentally, e‑waste and energy footprint require attention, with refurbishment as mitigation. Politically, connectivity plans and procurement policies shape feasibility.

Implications

Improved access enables broader participation and course persistence. Talent can develop beyond capital cities. Low‑resource models can inform deployments elsewhere.

Solutions

Establish device‑loan schemes, reliable campus Wi‑Fi and offline/low‑bandwidth modes. Turn on accessibility by default—captions, transcripts, text‑to‑speech—and ensure multilingual support.

Connection to Lecture Topics

Epistemology (access to knowledge), Logic (clear explanation), Concept of Human Being (dignity and inclusion).
